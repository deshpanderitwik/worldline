# Reality — a working map

Mastery is not control. It is **moving with the grain of each layer**.
Lower layers constrain higher ones. You cannot out-narrate physics, out-think biology, or out-hustle sleep.

---

## Layer 0 — Physics

**What's true.** Energy is conserved. Entropy increases. Time moves one way. Distance and force are real.

**What it demands.** Anything you want to maintain — a body, a relationship, a project, a room — costs continuous energy. Stop paying, and it decays. This is not a moral fact, just an accounting one.

**Lever.** Compounding. Small inputs sustained over long time beat large inputs applied briefly.

**Trap.** Believing willpower or cleverness exempts you. It does not.

---

## Layer 1 — Body

**What's true.** You are a metabolism, not a mind that has a body. Cognition, mood, and willpower are downstream of sleep, glucose, light, breath, and movement.

**What it demands.**
- Sleep before everything. A bad night beats any productivity system.
- Sunlight in the morning, darkness at night.
- Move daily, lift weekly. Tissue you don't load, you lose.
- Breathe through the nose. Slower than feels natural.
- Eat protein and plants. Most other rules are fashion.

**Lever.** The body responds to *signal*, not effort. Consistent small signals reshape it; heroic episodes don't.

**Trap.** Treating the body as a vehicle for the mind. It is the other way around.

---

## Layer 2 — Attention

**What's true.** Attention is the substance of your life. What you attend to, you become. There is no neutral viewing.

**What it demands.** Curate inputs ruthlessly. The feed is not free; it is paid for in the shape of your mind.

**Lever.** Single-tasking. Long-form attention is now a rare resource — which means it compounds faster than it used to.

**Trap.** Confusing stimulation with engagement, or information with understanding.

---

## Layer 3 — Emotion

**What's true.** Emotions are data about your state and your environment, not commands. They pass through the body in roughly 90 seconds if not rehearsed.

**What it demands.** Feel the sensation; don't narrate it. The story you tell about the feeling is what makes it stick.

**Lever.** Naming. "I am noticing anger" loosens what "I am angry" grips.

**Trap.** Suppression (pushing down) and identification (becoming the feeling) are both worse than witnessing.

---

## Layer 4 — Belief

**What's true.** You act from your model of reality, not reality. The model is always wrong somewhere.

**What it demands.** Hold beliefs loosely enough to update. Hold values tightly enough to act.

**Lever.** Calibration. Notice when you're surprised — that's where your map is wrong, and where learning is cheapest.

**Trap.** Confusing the strength of a belief with its accuracy. Conviction is not evidence.

---

## Layer 5 — Other people

**What's true.** Humans run on status, trust, reciprocity, and narrative. These are not flaws to transcend; they are the medium.

**What it demands.**
- Tell the truth, especially when costly. Compounds into trust, which is the rarest currency.
- Keep small promises. Reputation is built on the boring ones.
- Assume good faith until shown otherwise; then update fast.

**Lever.** Generosity that doesn't track. Most people keep ledgers; not keeping one is a quiet superpower.

**Trap.** Optimizing for being seen as good rather than being good. The first reads as performance and erodes trust.

---

## Layer 6 — Symbols

**What's true.** Money, institutions, brands, contracts, language — these are shared hallucinations that nonetheless move atoms. Consensus reality is real because we act on it.

**What it demands.** Learn the rules of the games you're in. Decide which to play seriously, which to play loosely, which to refuse.

**Lever.** Owning rather than renting. Equity, skills, relationships, and a body that works compound. Salaries, status, and applause do not.

**Trap.** Mistaking the symbol for the thing. Money is not wealth; titles are not competence; words are not understanding.

---

## Layer 7 — Meaning

**What's true.** Meaning is not found; it is made by what you give your life to. The universe does not assign it.

**What it demands.** Pick something worth the cost of your attention. Let it be hard.

**Lever.** Service to something larger than the self quiets the self more reliably than any practice aimed at the self.

**Trap.** Searching for a meaning that will arrive pre-validated. None will.

---

## Operating principles (cross-layer)

1. **Substrate first.** Sleep, light, food, movement, breath. If these are off, nothing above works.
2. **Slow is fast.** Most things you want require years. Pick directions you can sustain.
3. **Reduce, then optimize.** Subtraction is almost always undervalued.
4. **Make it a practice, not a project.** Projects end; practices compound.
5. **Reality has the final vote.** When the map and the territory disagree, redraw the map.

---

## How to consult this

- When stuck, ask: *which layer is this actually in?* Most "mind" problems are body problems. Most "people" problems are belief problems. Most "meaning" problems are attention problems.
- When something isn't working, check downward. The fix is usually one or two layers below where the symptom shows up.
- Revise this document. A map you don't edit is a map you've stopped using.
